If a PDF has come from a scanner, it's almost guaranteed to have at least one of these problems: a sideways page, a blank page from the scanner feed, or pages in the wrong order. None of these require redoing the whole document — just organizing the pages that exist.

## 1. Get a page-by-page view first

Before changing anything, open the PDF in a tool that shows page thumbnails. This lets you see the whole document at a glance and spot:

- Pages rotated 90° or 180°
- Blank pages (common with double-sided scans of single-sided documents)
- Duplicate pages
- Pages that are out of order

## 2. Rotate pages

1. Open [ClarixPDF's organize tool](https://clarixpdf.com/organize).
2. Upload the PDF — you'll see thumbnails of every page.
3. Click the rotate icon on any sideways page.
4. Repeat for each affected page.

## 3. Reorder and delete pages

- **Reorder:** drag thumbnails into the correct sequence.
- **Delete:** select blank or duplicate pages and remove them.

## 4. Save and verify

After making changes:

1. Save/export the reorganized PDF.
2. Scroll through the entire result — not just the pages you changed.
3. Confirm the page count matches your expectations.

## When to extract pages instead

If you only need a few pages from a much larger document, it's often faster to extract just those pages into a new file rather than deleting all the others. Most organize tools offer an "extract" option for exactly this case.
