Whether you're combining scanned receipts, assembling a contract package, or putting together a report with separate appendices, merging PDFs is one of the most common document tasks — and one of the easiest to get wrong if your tool reorders pages or strips bookmarks.

## 1. Gather and rename your files first

Before uploading anything, rename your files so they sort in the order you want the final document to read. A simple numbering scheme works well:

```
01-cover-letter.pdf
02-resume.pdf
03-references.pdf
```

Most merge tools combine files in the order you upload or arrange them, so starting organized saves a step later.

## 2. Upload and arrange

1. Go to [ClarixPDF's merge tool](https://clarixpdf.com/merge).
2. Drag in all the files you want to combine.
3. Reorder them by dragging thumbnails — double-check the order against your plan from step 1.
4. Remove any blank or duplicate pages you spot in the previews.

## 3. Merge and verify

Click merge, then open the resulting file and check:

- **Page order** matches what you intended.
- **Page count** matches the sum of your individual files.
- **Bookmarks/outline**, if your source files had them, carried over correctly.

## 4. Name and save the final file

Use a clear, dated filename for the merged document — something like `proposal-acme-2026-06-03.pdf` avoids the dreaded `final_v3_FINAL_actuallyfinal.pdf` situation.

## When you might need something more than a basic merge

If you're regularly assembling the same type of document, it's worth building a template and only swapping the pages that change — that's faster than merging from scratch each time.
