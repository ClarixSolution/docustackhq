Signing a PDF on an iPhone sounds simple until you actually try it. Here are three methods that genuinely work — no printer required.

## Method 1: Use the iPhone's built-in Markup tool (fastest for simple signatures)

iOS has had a built-in PDF signing tool for years and most people don't know about it.

1. Open the PDF in the **Files app** (or tap the attachment in Mail)
2. Tap the **pencil icon** in the top right to open Markup
3. Tap the **plus (+)** button at the bottom right → **Signature**
4. Draw your signature with your finger (or Apple Pencil)
5. Tap **Done** → drag and resize the signature to the right spot
6. Tap **Done** again to save

**Pros:** No apps to install, completely free, works offline.
**Cons:** Signature is drawn with your finger so it may not look as clean. No option to type your name or upload an image of your signature.

## Method 3: Use the Files app with a third-party PDF app (best for regular signers)

If you sign documents regularly, a dedicated PDF app is worth having on your phone.

1. Download a PDF app from the App Store (Adobe Acrobat, PDF Expert, or similar)
2. Open your PDF in the app
3. Look for the **Sign** or **Signature** tool in the toolbar
4. Create and place your signature
5. Save and share

**Pros:** Best for frequent signers, works offline, integrates with cloud storage.
**Cons:** Some apps require a subscription for the signature feature.

## Is an iPhone signature legally valid?

For most everyday documents — freelance contracts, NDAs, rental agreements, internal approvals — yes. Electronic signatures are recognized under ESIGN in the US, eIDAS in the EU, and similar laws in most countries.

For high-stakes documents (property sales, wills, certain government forms), check with the requesting party what signature standard they need before relying on a mobile e-signature.

## Tips for a clean signature on a small screen

- **Use an Apple Pencil** if you have one — it gives far more control than a finger
- **Zoom in** before placing a typed signature in a small field
- **Save your signature** in the tool if it offers that option — saves time on the next document
- **Screenshot and check** the final placement before downloading
