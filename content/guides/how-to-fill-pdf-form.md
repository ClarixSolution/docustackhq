A surprising number of "print, fill out by hand, and scan back" forms can just be filled in on screen instead — whether or not the PDF was originally built with fillable fields.

## 1. Check if the form already has fillable fields

Open the PDF and click around where you'd normally write. If boxes highlight and a cursor appears, the form already has interactive fields — you can just click into each one and type, then tab between fields.

If clicking does nothing, the PDF is a flat document and needs the next step.

## 2. Fill a non-interactive form

1. Open [ClarixPDF's fill tool](https://clarixpdf.com/fill).
2. Upload the PDF.
3. Use the text tool to click anywhere on the page and type — this places a text box you can position over each blank line or box.
4. For checkboxes, use the checkmark tool to place a mark in the right spot.
5. Repeat for each field, then save.

## 3. Tips for a clean result

- **Match the font size** to the surrounding text where possible.
- **Zoom in** before placing text in tight fields so it lines up correctly.
- **Save your progress** periodically if the form is long.

## 4. Adding a signature to a filled form

If the form needs a signature, see our guide on [how to e-sign a PDF](/guides/how-to-esign-pdf) for adding a typed or drawn signature to the completed form.

## 5. Submitting the completed form

Once filled, most forms can be emailed as a PDF attachment directly or uploaded to a portal. Keep a copy of the completed form for your own records before sending.
