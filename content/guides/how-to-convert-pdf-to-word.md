PDF-to-Word conversion has a reputation for mangling formatting. It doesn't have to be that way.

## 1. Understand what converts cleanly

- **Native PDFs** (from Word, Google Docs) convert well.
- **Scanned PDFs** need OCR before they become editable text.
- **Complex layouts** will need manual cleanup after conversion.

## 2. Convert PDF to Word

1. Open ClarixPDF's convert tool at clarixpdf.com/convert and select PDF to Word.
2. Upload your file. Enable OCR if it's a scan.
3. Download the .docx and open it in Word or Google Docs.

## 3. Clean up the converted document

Check that headings use proper styles, look for stray page breaks, and re-check tables for any cells that shifted during conversion.

## 4. Converting back to PDF

Export directly from Word or use ClarixPDF's Word to PDF tool. Open the result and verify fonts embedded correctly.
